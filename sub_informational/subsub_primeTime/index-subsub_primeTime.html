Prime Time

“Biological Prime Time”, as a term taken from user FromSerigo’s Youtube video “The *Huge* Problem With Timeblocking: Here’s What I Do Instead” is a section of time in one’s day where they have the most focus—ergo, when they should be doing the majority of their work. I think that it’s one thing to simply understand the way something’s explained to you, but I think that, in how I’m using FromSergio’s “Biological Prime Time”, it’s better that I bring attention to a more generalized form of his topic—that being working with your own strengths and weaknesses.

As a collective, I think that one of the things many of us struggle with is trying to understand our own strengths and weaknesses in a world that feels so intent on containing us in whatever societally-fabricated boxes it pleases. It remains important that, before you engage with “established” time-saving techniques—like FromSeriogo’s Biological Prime Time—you have a good understanding of how you operate when you work.

[img = screenshot from video]


What affects your ability to do what you must do?
When do you have to do it?
Where should you be?

That last question, especially, is very important to me, as I feel like someone’s environment plays a big role in dictating how they approach the tasks that they need to complete. For instance, in my own room, I have posters, artwork, and other pieces of colorful merchandise that I felt would bring my room life hung up on my wall, making the space feel more lived in; I didn’t feel like I was in dark corner, hunched over my table and slowly working away on my work.

[image of room]

I like to think about the “dressing-up” of my room as an attempt by me to “set myself up for success”—something that I think is not only crucial to managing your own time, but is also one of the major driving factors of FromSergio’s video, especially when he explains the importance of “protecting your prime time”. Setting oneself up for success can take many different forms, like demonstrated in FromSergio’s video, where he says that he, “[…]alway[s] schedule[s] things that are flexible, like non-important meetings, doctor’s appointments, and gym sessions outside of [my prime time] […]”. To you, dear reader, setting yourself up for success might not look like either one of ours’—either adjusting the visual fidelity of a space or completing tasks within your peak hours—though your attempts should, no matter what, still be in service of your own benefits.

I want to go back to that last question from that set of three questions I had proposed before,


Where should you be?

as I feel that it has a sort-of correlation with Biological Prime Time—a “Biological Prime Space”, If you will. From my experience, where you physically situate yourself not only influences the way you work but also the type of work that you do; maybe you write better in a quiet library, or paint better in a bustling studio. No matter what you do, it’s important that you are, like before, aware of what potentially is and is not beneficial towards your current goals, as, in this case, choosing, for instance, to write in a place that you paint better in may set yourself up to not complete the things you wanted to get done.

On the topic of “Biological Prime Spaces”, it’s important to consider the options you have when picking out where to situate yourself. In relation to the goal(s) you want to complete, ask yourself,

Do I need to be alone?
Does the space need to be public?
What other tasks may I complete in this space? Is that important to think about?
Do I have access to the resources I need?
Can I get to and return from this space in a reasonable amount of time?

Again, that last question, especially, is noteworthy—your own means of travel very much dictate the spaces that you work in. No matter where you choose, it’s important to, as much as you can, make your own workspace your own—if you’re gonna be spending the energy in your Biological Prime Time in a biological prime space, you might as well deliberately try to push the ever-present force of time in your favour. Honour your body’s rhythms and needs—especially physically—and you’ll put yourself in a better spot to honour your time-consuming ambitions.
